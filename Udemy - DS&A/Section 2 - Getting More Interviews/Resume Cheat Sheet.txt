✅ Use a pre-designed resume template -  Resumemaker.online is one option
✅ Make the resume fit on 1 page
✅ Include words from job description
✅ Include company name you are applying to
✅ Does your first item on your resume reflect what they are looking for?
✅ Experience titles demonstrate value - Titles and listing skills, key items are often more important than where you worked. Also the most crucial skills should be listed first.
✅ Do you have an online link?
✅ Remove the word “I”
✅ No buzzwords describing how great you are - don't tell the recruiter HOW to feel about you.. use action words. 
✅ Are you using Action words? What did you actually do?
✅ Measure everything in terms of impact, don’t just describe your responsibilities
✅ Technical Knowledge/Skills should include what they are looking for. Only show years if it is impressive
✅ Include only sections/items that are impressive: Experience, Projects, Education, Technical Skills
✅ No typos or bad grammar


Try tools like Jobscan where you paste your resume and paste job description, and it will tell you how well your resume does & give tips.
